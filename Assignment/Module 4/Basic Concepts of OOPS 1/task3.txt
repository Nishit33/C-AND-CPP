	3. What is the difference between OOP and POP? 

	->The key diffrence between object-oriented programming and procedure oriented programming lie in their 
	approach to program design , data managment and structure.

	Pop - it focused on the steps involved in solving a problem and follows a top-down approach.
	data is often trated as seprate from the procedure that operate on it.


	oop - models real world entities as object which have both data attributes and behaviour method it focuses
		on the relationships between object and how they interact with each other.
		oop promotes code reusability , modularity and maintainbility through concepts like 
		inheritance , polymorphism and encapsulation.
